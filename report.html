P.S.

It is common and preferable to have more than one system in a house. This is especially true when their are multiple levels in the home. This would result in having one furnace upstairs and one downstairs.

This is preferable because the system will heat better and more efficiently since air doesn't have to travel so far to heat the entire home. Also, if one fails then you still have heat to one part of the house. Problems in general are easier to diagnose and isolate as either being downstais or upstairs.

Ductwork could likely be put underneath the house in the crawlspace. This would also be a fine place to put a furnace if there is adequate space to do so, and it has it's benefits, since ductwork coming from the furnace would be easier to install

If you decided to put the furnace in the garage or closet, this would make the furnace easier to access for anyone servicing it in the future, but it would require holes to be drilled for the ductwork coming into the house, which could be problematic

If you chose to put it in the crawlspace, the only necessary holes would be those in the floor for the actual vents themselves

It is worth considering putting it next to the water heater, which is a common practice, since they could then share the gas line and flue. Running a new gas line and flue could be problematic
